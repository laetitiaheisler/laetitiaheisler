Laetitia Heisler is a French-German visual artist based in Berlin. After studying dance at the Conservatory of Southern France and performing as a singer in a rock band, she turned to analogue photography and has since worked across experimental image-making, textile, and installation.

Her practice is rooted in the body as an archive of experiences. From self-portraiture to darkroom experimentation, she explores the latent spaces between control and surrender, presence and trace.

Heisler has exhibited in solo and group shows across Europe, with a particular presence in northern Germany and Edinburgh. Her analogue double exposures have gained recognition within the analogue community, and she teaches workshops in darkrooms across France and Germany. She is currently developing installations that extend the photographic medium into three-dimensional space.
