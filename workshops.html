Laetitia Heisler teaches workshops in darkrooms across France and Germany, sharing her expertise in analogue photography, double exposure techniques, and darkroom experimentation. Her pedagogical approach is rooted in the body as an archive of experiences, guiding participants through the latent spaces between control and surrender.

  • Darkroom Experimentation

    An immersive workshop exploring the possibilities of analogue photography in the darkroom. Participants learn double exposure techniques, chemical manipulation, and experimental image-making processes.

  • Self-Portraiture & the Body

    A workshop focused on self-portraiture as a tool for exploring identity, memory, and the body as an archive. Techniques include film manipulation, long exposures, and intimate camera work.

  • Analogue Photography Fundamentals

    Introduction to analogue photography: from loading film to developing negatives and making prints. Suitable for beginners who want to discover the tactile process of film photography.

  • From 2D to 3D: Expanding the Photographic Medium

    A workshop exploring how photographic images can extend into three-dimensional space through textile, installation, and mixed media approaches.
